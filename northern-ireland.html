Arrival & Belfast

Thursday · 25 June 2026

19:00

Flight

Gatwick → Belfast City (easyJet)

~1hr 20min. Arrives 20:30. Belfast City Airport is 10 minutes from the centre — unlike International, which is a 40-minute schlep.

Not flying from London? easyJet also operate direct routes to Belfast City from Bristol (~1hr 15min), Manchester (~1hr 5min) and Edinburgh (~45min) — check times to join the group on the ground Thursday evening.

~£70

21:15

Transfer

Metro 600 bus to Cathedral Quarter

Then walk to hotel. ~£2.60.

~£3

21:30

Check-In

easyHotel Belfast

Cathedral Quarter. Twin rooms.

±£59 / night

22:00

Pub — Grade I Listed optional

The Crown Liquor Saloon

Victorian gin palace, 1885. Ornate snugs, gas lamps, stained glass. Owned by the National Trust. One of the best pub rooms in Britain.

23:00

Pub Crawl optional

Cathedral Quarter — the circuit

Duke of York (cobbled alley, live trad) → The Dirty Onion (1780s warehouse, courtyard) → Kelly's Cellars (1720, Belfast's oldest). Let it go from there. Dan's tied to a lamppost in a tutu overnight.

02

The Mournes

Friday · 26 June 2026

08:30

Breakfast

Full Ulster Fry

Soda bread, potato bread, sausage, rasher, black pudding, egg, hair of the dog? Once we're done we go find where we left Dan.

09:00

Transport

Hire vehicle → Newcastle, Co. Down

Two 7-seaters split across the group. Enterprise at Belfast City Airport looks cheapest. 55-minute drive — A24 via Ballynahinch, past drumlins and the edge of the Mournes.

~£25 pp

11:00

Adventure Activity optional

Coasteering — Mourne Coast

2.5 hrs with Mourne Activities. Sea cliffs, cave swims, rock jumping. Wetsuits, helmets, guides provided. This is the bit Dan talks about in the speech.

~£45

14:00

Lunch & Recovery

Newcastle seafront

Fish and chips to aid recovery. Look at the mountains. Decompress. Think deep thoughts. Tease Dan.

15:30

Adventure Hike optional

Slieve Donard — 850m

Highest point in Northern Ireland. Return trail from Newcastle town, ~4 hrs. Views across to Scotland and the Isle of Man on a clear day. Optional — legs may vote no after coasteering.

Free

19:30

Supper + Bed

Back to Belfast

Supper, a few pints, early(-ish) bed. Ready for the Causeway.

±£59 / night

03

Causeway Coast

Saturday · 27 June 2026

08:30

Drive

Belfast → Causeway Coast

1hr 10min. The route leaves Belfast and then opens out onto the Antrim plateau. Increasingly dramatic.

10:30

Vertiginous optional

Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge

30 metres above the Atlantic. The bridge swings. The island smells of seabird. Needs advance booking — it sells out.

£13

12:00

Lunch

Bushmills Inn

Eat in Bushmills village before the distillery tour.

14:00

World's Oldest Whiskey

Old Bushmills Distillery — Tour & Tasting

Licensed since 1608. The tour is genuinely good — an active distillery, not a museum. Ends with a tasting. The 1608 anniversary edition is meant to be exceptional. ~1 hour.

£15

16:00

UNESCO World Heritage Site

Giant's Causeway

40,000 interlocking basalt hexagons. Free to walk the columns — parking and visitor centre costs £5 per car. Leave 1.5 hrs. Light will be golden and the worst of the crowds should have buggered off.

£5 / car

18:30

Drive Back

Causeway Coast → Belfast

~1hr 15min via the A26. Return hire cars. Check back in to Belfast hotel.

20:30

Big Night

The Merchant Hotel Cocktail Bar

One of the best cocktail bars in Ireland. Victorian banking hall, extraordinary gin list. Feels special without being precious. From here: Linen Hall Bar → Boxed → let the night — not Dan — decide.

23:59

Bed

easyHotel Belfast

Cathedral Quarter. Twin rooms. Arguably the best £59 you've ever spent.

±£59 / night
